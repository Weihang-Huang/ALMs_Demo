API configuration

Your key stays in your browser session and is only forwarded to the model endpoint. It is never logged or stored on the server.

Authorial model slots

Each slot = one author's language model. The model whose perplexity is lowest on the questioned text is the predicted author. In this demo each slot is conditioned by a short public-domain style primer (editable below); in a production ALMs pipeline each slot would point at its own fine-tuned model.

CNLL heatmap (Fig 3 / 7)

Rows = tokens (top-to-bottom in reading order). Columns = candidate authors, ordered by their overall rank (best on the left, highlighted in red). Cells are colored by the word's CNLL for that author: blue = evidence for, red = evidence against. The bar chart below shows mean CNLL per author – a large negative bar for the winner confirms the attribution is token-supported, not driven by one or two outliers.
